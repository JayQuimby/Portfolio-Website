Interests

My career objective is to leverage my skills and knowledge in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) to contribute towards the development of safe and effective Artificial General Intelligence. I am passionate about growing as a technician and future leader in this exciting field.

I have advanced Python skills for scripting and ML applications. I also have a deep understanding of computers, algorithms, AI, ML, and evolutionary computation methods. Additionally, I have experience with game engines like Unity and Godot for AI/ML training. I have completed projects using techniques like web scraping and data processing.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Auburn University in 2022. In high school, I received an award for Outstanding Achievement in Computer Science. Throughout my education, I gained expertise in AI, evolutionary algorithms, and machine learning, including multi-objective optimization and representation space exploration. For example, I created a KNN-Centroid algorithm to predict hiking routes and implemented reinforcement learning in a 3D game environment to train agents.

My work experience includes serving as an AI Analyst at Deloitte, where I developed large language models and worked on internal ML initiatives. I have also been the website manager for Stemplus, an online education company, where I focused on developing VR/AR solutions for distance learning. Additionally, I am the President and Co-Founder of my own organization, Ellipsium Industries LLC.

In my free time, I enjoy playing intramural sports, video editing and animation, as well as running my investment club. I have also achieved Eagle Scout rank in the Boy Scouts, which speaks to my ambition and leadership abilities.

The current project that I am working on involves creating a video game that uses Langchain to serve LLM outputs as the storyline of the game. This system highly relies on prompt engineering, back-end development, and game design.

Background

My journey in the world of computing began at the age of 12 when I delved into the intricate world of hardware by researching and ordering parts to build my first computer from scratch.

Throughout my high school years, my enthusiasm for technology only grew. I honed my skills by delving into the realms of ethical hacking, jail-breaking iPhones/iPod touches, and even ran a successful screen repair business at 16 years old.

My formal studies in Computer Science commenced during my junior year of high school, and I soon found myself attending hackathons, where I showcased my skills. In My senior year of High School my efforts were recognized by the prestigious Outstanding Achievement in Computer Science award from my teacher.

Embarking on higher education at Auburn University, I accelerated through my studies, graduating in three years with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. Much of my college experience was spent doing hands on projects, such as creating a primitive messaging app and engaging in high-level planning using the Waterfall method for application development.

Hackathon competitions became a playground for my innovative spirit. I crafted a custom recommender system, employing a KNN-centroid algorithm to match users with hiking trails tailored to their preferences. Additionally, I tackled Evolutionary Computing methods to optimize a Pac-Man player, and I engaged into AI by developing a Tetris playing system that beat the game.

In the professional arena, my role as a Website Manager at Stemplus tasked me with optimizing and enhancing the website's functionality. I delved into cutting-edge technologies like AR rooms in Mozilla hubs to create engaging virtual classrooms, showcasing my skills not only in website management but also in graphic design.

My journey continued at Deloitte, where I served as an Applied AI Engineer. Working with AWS, I contributed to the creation of enterprise-level LLMs and delved into the healthcare sector, developing demos and proofs of concept for innovative healthcare applications. Despite my impactful contributions, unforeseen circumstances led to a change, and I was laid off due to a lack of work internally.

Outside of formal commitments, my free time is a playground for innovation. I've developed an AI algorithm that automatically colors black and white images, experimented with auto-encoders for image processing, and even created a predictive model for NCAA Football games, complete with an interactive website for sharing insights.
